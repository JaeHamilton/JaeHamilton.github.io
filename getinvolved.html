Wish List

There is so much more that goes into surviving abuse than experiencing the violent relationship itself.

Many survivors face additional barriers to safety when they are without the resources to get their basic needs met.
This includes, but is not limited to, access to food and personal hygiene products.
Beyond fleeing unsafe situations, our program participants often encounter ongoing, unmet needs for transportation.
Survivors attending various appointments with outside organizations, social service agencies, court dates and therapy sessions, for example,
can become overwhelmed by their lack of viable transportation.
All of these issues are exacerbated by the high cost of living and general effects of gentrification in the Bay Area.

These items can help minimize some of these barriers
and support survivors of domestic violence with the long-term change they strive to achieve:

Clipper cards, loaded increments of $10.00

    Why we need it:
  • they help us relocate participants who need
    to leave the city due to emergencies or relocation
  • they help our participants get around the city
    (and the Bay Area in general) as they attend appointments, court dates, and/or work

    • VISA/Mastercard/American Express Gift cards

      Why we need it:
  • these cards allow us to be adaptive as we support our participants
  • these cards can be used to purchase participant prescriptions,
    filing fees for court, document reproduction, therapy services,
    participant stipends and training supplies

    • Gift cards for Walgreens, Safeway, Whole Foods, TJ Maxx, Macy’s, Ross, local restaurants, etc.

  • a portion of our budget must be unrestricted so we can respond to unique, one-time, unplanned and emerging participant need
